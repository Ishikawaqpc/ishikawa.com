Six Sigma

Six Sigma (6σ) is a set of techniques and tools for process improvement. It seeks to improve the quality of the output of a process by identifying and removing the causes of defects and minimizing variability in manufacturing and business processes. It uses a set of quality management methods, mainly empirical, statistical methods, and creates a special infrastructure of people within the organization who are experts in these methods. Each Six Sigma project carried out within an organization follows a defined sequence of steps and has specific value targets, for example: reduce process cycle time, reduce pollution, reduce costs, increase customer satisfaction, and increase profits.

CONSULTING

Business Transformation

Change management strategy which has the aim to align People, Process and Technology initiatives of a company more closely with its business strategy and vision.

DMAIC

DMAIC (Define Measure Analyze Improve Control) is a data-driven quality strategy that is used to improve processes by reducing variation within manufacturing processes.

Value Stream Mapping (VSM)

Analyze and improve flow of material and information required to produce a product. VSM identifies bottlenecks, waste and helps streamline processes.

Critical To Quality (CTQ)

Identification of specific, measurable critical to quality (CTQ) characteristics is essential for meaningful and measurable business process improvement.

Design Of Experiments (DOE)

Determine the relationship between factors affecting a process and its output, establish cause-and-effect relationships and manage process inputs to optimize the output.

Project Management

Initiating, planning, executing, controlling, and closing the work to achieve specific quality and process improvement goals and meet specific success criteria at the specified time.
